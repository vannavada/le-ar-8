# le-ar-8 — Logo Concepts & Brand Documentation

Two logo directions under consideration for le-ar-8 (lear8.com). This file
records the concept, rationale, palette, and typography for each, plus the
brand-name story that ties them together. Source: a generated mockup
(`original-concepts-reference.png`) reinterpreted as editable SVG with the
correct "le-ar-8" spelling.

## The brand name story (applies to both concepts)

**le-ar-8 reads as "layer 8."**

In networking, the OSI model has 7 layers — the machine stack, from physical
wires up to the application. "Layer 8" is industry slang for the layer that
sits above all of them: **the human layer.** The person. The user. The one who
actually thinks, decides, and writes.

That is exactly what le-ar-8 is — a personal content hub where a human writes
for humans. So the name isn't just a phonetic play on a domain; it's the whole
thesis of the site: **le-ar-8 = Layer 8 = the human layer above the machine.**

Both logo concepts encode this idea differently:
- Concept 1 makes it literal — a human "8" sitting on top of the machine layers.
- Concept 3 makes it cultural — the terminal/command-line aesthetic of someone
  who lives close to the machine but is unmistakably the human operating it.

## Concept 1 — "The Human Element"

**File:** `concept-1-human-element.svg` (preview: `concept-1-human-element-preview.png`)

### The idea
A figure-8 / infinity mark formed above a stack of horizontal bars. The bars
are the OSI layers (the machine stack); the 8 above them is Layer 8 — the human.
The infinity reading also suggests continuity and ongoing creation. In the
original mockup the loops are organic ribbon strands in magenta and cyan; the
SVG is a cleaner geometric interpretation of that ribbon idea.

### Tone
Vibrant, technical, human-meets-machine. More expressive and colorful than
Concept 3. Reads as a tech-forward personal brand with personality.

### Typography
**Montserrat** (or Inter) — bold geometric sans for the wordmark.

### Palette
| Role | Name | Hex |
|---|---|---|
| Primary dark | Deep Slate | `#2F4F4F` |
| Accent 1 | Muted Magenta | `#D81B60` |
| Accent 2 | Electric Cyan | `#00E5FF` |
| Accent 3 | Tech Blue | `#1976D2` |
| Light | Light Gray | `#F5F5F5` |

### Honest notes
- Multi-color and energetic — strong as a standalone brand mark, but it pulls
  in a different direction from the restrained Linear/Paradigm + Instrument
  Serif aesthetic the site itself is currently built in (see clash note below).
- The geometric figure-8 is a simplified take on the original's organic ribbon.
  A professional designer could render the ribbon weave properly.

## Concept 3 — "The Terminal Dot"

**File:** `concept-3-terminal.svg` (preview: `concept-3-terminal-preview.png`)

### The idea
A code-bracket terminal prompt: `[ /8 ]` with a blinking-cursor block. Evokes
the command line — the environment of someone who works close to the machine.
The `8` inside the brackets is Layer 8, the human at the prompt. Monospace
throughout, like a terminal. This is the concept the "le-ar-8 ≈ layer-8"
wordplay fits most naturally — it's understated, clever, and code-literate.

### Tone
Minimal, technical, confident, a little insider. Restrained where Concept 1 is
expressive. Reads as a sharp, technical, no-nonsense personal brand.

### Typography
**Space Mono** (or Roboto Mono) — terminal monospace for both mark and wordmark.

### Palette
| Role | Name | Hex |
|---|---|---|
| Primary dark | Charcoal | `#1A1A1A` |
| Accent (chosen) | Neon Teal | `#00D9CC` |
| Accent (alt) | Matrix Green | `#00FF41` |
| Light | Terminal White | `#FFFFFF` |
| Muted | Slate Gray | `#708090` |

*The SVG uses Neon Teal as the accent (calmer, more elegant than Matrix Green,
which leans "hacker cliché"). Matrix Green is kept as an alternate.*

### Honest notes
- Cleanest of the two to execute and the most distinctive. The monospace +
  bracket mark is memorable and ownable.
- Neon Teal is closer to the site's existing restraint than Concept 1's
  magenta/cyan, so this concept clashes less with the current design direction.

## Important: logo vs. current site aesthetic (decide before committing a logo)

The site as currently built leans **Linear/Paradigm — cool, restrained,
editorial**, with **Instrument Serif** display type, a single accent color, and
per-section accent colors. Both logo concepts are more **vibrant and
tech-flavored** than that — especially Concept 1.

This is a real tension to resolve, not a detail:
- If the logo is **Concept 3 (Terminal/monospace)**, there's a philosophical
  clash worth noting: the site uses an elegant *serif* (Instrument Serif), the
  logo uses *monospace*. Serif-elegant and terminal-mono are two different brand
  personalities. Pick one as dominant, or deliberately use the contrast.
- If the logo is **Concept 1**, its magenta/cyan palette needs reconciling with
  the site's restrained single-accent approach.

**Recommendation:** choose the logo direction first, then let it inform (or
correct) the site's visual direction so the two cohere — rather than having an
editorial-serif site with an unrelated tech logo bolted on. This is a branding
decision for the owner; flagged here so it's conscious.
